Key finding

  • Single-expiry median AUM cap: $3.7M. At the canonical EXP-1220 single-expiration Monday entry, the median Monday in 2020-2025 supports a book of $3.7M at 10% participation.
  • Top-3 staggered median AUM cap: $5.7M. Spreading entries across the three highest-volume expiries in the 21-45 DTE window multiplies median capacity by 1.5×.
  • Feasibility with top-3 staggering: $100M is tradable on 3% of Mondays, $200M on 0%, $500M on 0%. A production staggered strategy could comfortably run a $1M book with almost no forced-downsize days (the p5-Monday capacity is the binding constraint).
  • AUM ceiling for 'always-tradable' (95%+ feasibility): single_expiry=< $50M; top2_expiries=< $50M; top3_expiries=< $50M; all_expiries=< $50M;

Methodology & caveats

  • Volume as a capacity proxy. IronVault's option_daily.open_interest is 100% NULL for SPY puts. The participation-rate market-impact model uses daily volume as its denominator anyway, so this is the right metric for 'how big a trade can I do today without moving the book'.
  • Entry cadence assumption. Every Monday between 2020-01-01 and 2025-12-31, I look up SPY puts with DTE in [21, 45] — matches the EXP-1220 28-DTE target ± 2 weeks. For each expiry I find the strike closest to 5% OTM of the real Yahoo spot and read that strike's daily volume from IronVault.
  • Multi-expiry staggering. The 'top2' and 'top3' variants SUM the daily volumes across the 2 or 3 expiries with the highest-volume short strikes. The assumption is that each expiry is an independent liquidity pool — which is correct for different-dated contracts — and that we can allocate trades proportionally across them. 'top_all' sums every expiry in the DTE window, usually 3-5 per Monday.
  • AUM conversion. contracts_needed = 2% × AUM / $500 per contract. A trade is feasible if contracts_needed ≤ 10% × sum_volume across the chosen expiries.
  • Honest limits of this model. Volume can vary day-to-day by 5-10×; a single low-volume Monday can force a trade delay on a capacity-constrained book. The p5 AUM cap column is the relevant number for worst-case capacity planning. The median is a fair number for average-case planning.
  • What this does NOT model. (a) market-impact non-linearity (10% participation is a rough cap — real impact cost scales super-linearly above that), (b) portfolio-margin haircuts that reduce effective AUM, (c) adverse selection when trading across multiple expiries simultaneously from a single account, (d) broker / exchange per-account order-size limits.
