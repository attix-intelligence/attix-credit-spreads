EXP-3309 — Liquidity-weighted entry timing

Decompose EXP-2420 round-trip cost into per-leg entry + exit, sweep entry-window timing (open / mid / pre-close), hold exit at pre-close (production EXP-2470 default). Identify whether moving entries from the open into the pre-close window delivers material net-Sharpe savings on v8a. 2026-05-09 12:17

Rule Zero. Round-trip $ baseline pulled from EXP-2420 (real IronVault p25 spreads + Yahoo 90d ADV at 3× leverage): bid-ask $4,175, slippage $9,756, commission $8,273. Per-leg costs = round-trip / 2. v8a anchor: gross SR 6.83, gross CAGR 284.4% (EXP-2600 8-stream LW @ target_vol=0.18), implied ann vol 19.71%.

3. Reading the result

  • Pre-close entry saves ~939 on bid-ask (94 bps) and ~2488 on slippage (249 bps) per round-trip relative to open entry, holding exit fixed at pre-close.
  • Fill rate is informational, not a drag line. At 85% mid-limit fill the unfilled 15% retries as market orders at the open-window spread — which is exactly the cost the open spread× factor already represents. Adding a separate "missed PnL" opportunity cost would double-count.
  • Net Sharpe lift vs EXP-2470 baseline: +0.174. EXP-2470 stack-B already captured the slippage half of pre-close savings on the exit leg; pre-close ENTRY harvests the remaining ~half on the entry leg. The marginal savings are real but small relative to overall drag.

4. Production recommendation

If the $942 per-trade PnL figure holds in live trading, scenario D (D: Pre-close entry / Pre-close exit (proposed)) is strictly dominant on this cost model. The two operational considerations not captured here:

  1. Signal staleness. Many of v8a's streams generate signals from morning IV / open prices; deferring entry by ~6 hours introduces signal decay we have not measured. EXP-2470 method-B implicitly assumes signal freshness loss is small.
  2. Adverse-selection in the closing auction. Closing-auction order flow is increasingly dominated by passive indexing rebalances, which are not noisy fills — but on rare event days (FOMC, NFP) the closing print has crossed-spread slippage that this scenario model does not capture.
