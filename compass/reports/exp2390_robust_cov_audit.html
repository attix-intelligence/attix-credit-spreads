EXP-2390 — Audit of EXP-2360 Robust Covariance Sharpe Numbers

Investigation of the suspicious Sharpe 11.7-13.7 (and one_factor 19.1) pooled OOS values · 2026-04-08 04:36

Rule Zero: reuses compass.exp2080_corr_regime.load_streams (cached 5-stream real-data cube) + XLF/XLI from compass.exp2160_high_capacity_alts. Only transformation is the XLF/XLI convert-to-daily method (smeared vs sparse).

ROOT CAUSE — NOT a Sharpe formula bug, NOT look-ahead

Look-ahead bias: NONE (verified by code inspection — train = df.iloc[i-TRAIN:i], test = df.iloc[i:i+TEST], covariance fit on train only, vol-target scale computed on train only).
Sharpe formula: CORRECT (mean/std × √252, cross- consistency check passes: μ·252 ÷ σ·√252 reproduces the reported Sharpe to 4 decimals).
Vol targeting: APPLIED (scaled = raw * target_vol / train_vol, clipped [0.1, 5.0]).

The bug: EXP-2360 builds the XLF/XLI streams via compass.exp2160_high_capacity_alts.trades_to_daily_pct, which UNIFORMLY SMEARS each ~30-day credit-spread P&L across the holding window. EXP-2160's own report flags this as "method-inflated on high-win-rate strategies due to P&L smearing across the holding period". XLF at 98.4% win rate has per-trade Sharpe 11.19 that inflates to a smeared daily Sharpe of ~27.22 (2.4× inflation).

When fed into Ledoit-Wolf / OAS / one-factor shrinkage covariance, the estimator sees XLF/XLI as "extraordinarily high-Sharpe, near-zero- vol, near-zero-correlation" streams and loads them heavily in the risk-parity weights. Vol-targeting then scales the resulting low-vol portfolio up to the 15% annual target, multiplying the inflated mean along with it.

VERDICT

The 11.73 / 13.73 Sharpe values are arithmetically correct on the smeared cube (no bugs, no look-ahead, no formula errors) but the smeared cube is NOT tradeable — it models each 30-day P&L as if it accrued smoothly every day. The HONEST pooled Sharpe on the sparse (exit-date) cube is in the 6.66-6.87 range, in line with the EXP-2110 (5.24) and EXP-2280 (4.43) baselines. Do NOT use EXP-2360 numbers in any deployment or investor materials.

4. Recommendation

1. EXP-2360 headline numbers should be marked STALE. 2. Re-run EXP-2360 with sparse XLF/XLI and republish. 3. Add a unit test to exp2160 that FAILS if the sleeve is consumed as a daily Sharpe target without the trade-level caveat. 4. The trade-level Sharpe (XLF 11.19, XLI 6.04) remains valid as a per-trade metric — it's the portfolio-level daily Sharpe on smeared streams that is misleading.
