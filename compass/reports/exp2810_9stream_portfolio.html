Verdict

  • DOES NOT SHIP. Failing gates: net Sharpe 2.337 < 6.0.
  • Vs EXP-2730 (net Sharpe 6.164 baseline). Static mix net Sharpe 2.337 trails the risk-parity v8a baseline by -3.827. The static mix does not expand the Sharpe buffer above the 6.0 ship gate.
  • Capacity binding stream: xlf_cs — at 100% of its $30.0M hard cap, the portfolio is $300.0M AUM. To scale beyond this, the xlf_cs weight must be cut OR its hard cap lifted (backfill / strike staggering).
  • SPY weekly contribution. At 15% weight and ~$7.6B hard cap, spy_wk contributes $1.1B of portfolio AUM headroom — 4× the $200M target. It is NOT the binding stream at this weight; the binding constraint is the old thin chains (XLF/XLI) which still cap the book. Reducing XLF/XLI further would unlock more headroom, but the task pinned them at 10%/3%.

Methodology & caveats

  • Static weights override risk parity. The MASTERPLAN directive fixes the mix explicitly because EXP-2740 showed that risk-parity over-weights XLI (the biggest single sensitivity breach). The LW+risk-parity column is shown for comparison only.
  • Drag recomputation rescales EXP-2420's per-stream dollar cost by new_weight / baseline_weight. QQQ and SPY-weekly lines are scaled from EXP-1220's per-unit (contracts × legs) cost using typical trade profiles (14/yr×2×4 for QQQ, 49/yr×2×4 for spy_wk). The total is a first-order approximation — the real number depends on fill mechanics that are out of scope.
  • Capacity math uses hard per-stream caps from earlier experiments (EXP-1770, 2060, 2160, 2260, 2610). Portfolio cap = min(stream_hard_cap / weight). The binding constraint is whichever stream fills first as total book grows.
  • Ship gate: net Sharpe ≥ 6.0 AND portfolio capacity ≥ $200M. Both must pass.
  • What this does NOT test: (a) correlation of the new spy_wk stream with the existing streams over the walk-forward window (already measured at |ρ| < 0.12 in EXP-2610, consistent here), (b) interaction effects of simultaneous parameter drift (covered in EXP-2740 OAT, not here), (c) forward-looking SLV/XLI capacity drift (they are small weights so the drift doesn't matter much).
