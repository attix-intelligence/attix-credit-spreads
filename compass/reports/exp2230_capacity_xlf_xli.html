EXP-2230 — 7-Stream Capacity Analysis (SPY + XLF + XLI)

Real IronVault option contract volumes · Yahoo Finance ADVs + prices · Square-root market-impact model (1% soft / 5% hard participation)

Hypothesis tested: "XLF and XLI add dramatic capacity because sector ETF options are deeply liquid." Honest finding: XLF and XLI options are liquid enough for mid-AUM deployment but their combined volume is only ~5 percent of SPY's. The capacity uplift from adding them is real but not dramatic — the pre-existing SPY bottleneck was never SPY options; it is the VIX / SLV / IWM sleeves. Splitting the credit-spread sleeve across 3 underliers mainly helps via execution diversification and lower market-impact per venue.

Method

  • Liquidity: IronVault option_daily × option_contracts aggregated per ticker, median daily total contract volume since 2024.
  • Notional: contracts/d × 100 × last Yahoo close.
  • Impact: square-root model, impact_bps = coeff · √participation, coeff 150 for options, 100 for equity, 120 for futures.
  • Soft cap: 1% of ADV. Hard cap: 5% of ADV.
  • Sharpe after impact: baseline × (alpha - impact·turnover)/alpha, 50 turns/yr.
  • AUM tiers: $10M, $50M, $100M, $500M, $1B.
  • Sweep: 3-variable grid over SPY/XLF/XLI fractions of the 60% credit-spread sleeve (step 0.1), other weights fixed at EXP-2140 values.
