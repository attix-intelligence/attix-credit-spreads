1 · Executive Summary

PilotAI Credit Spreads systematically harvests the Volatility Risk Premium (VRP) across 8 uncorrelated options streams — index put-credit spreads, sector ETF credit spreads, commodity calendars, and a cross-vol IV–RV book — with risk-parity weighting, a 3% trailing-drawdown circuit breaker, and a cost-aware execution stack that recovers 503 bps/yr versus a naive baseline. Every number in this deck is reproducible from the experiment registry.

What we are

  • Multi-stream VRP capture across 8 streams — only 18.7% of the edge comes from SPX-style put-credit (EXP-3151).
  • Defined-risk only. Spreads, calendars, and IV/RV pairs — no naked short premium.
  • Risk-parity weighted with Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage covariance (EXP-2360).
  • Hard-coded circuit breaker. 3% trailing flatten — validated 20-fold, zero false trips (EXP-2370).

What we are not

  • Not a tail-risk seller. We hedge with VIX calls (v5_hedge sleeve).
  • Not a high-frequency latency arbitrage play.
  • Not a leveraged short-vol fund — we de-lever in stress, base 1.0×.
  • Not claiming this is risk-free. See §4 for the cases where our DD claim does not hold.

2 · The Edge — why VRP, why now, why us

Why VRP exists

Listed-option implied volatility persistently exceeds subsequent realized volatility — 3–5 vol points on indices, 4–8 on sector ETFs. This is the cost of dealer hedging in an inventory-constrained market plus a structural insurance premium end-investors pay to transfer left-tail risk.

Refs: Bakshi & Kapadia (2003); Carr & Wu (2009); BIS Quarterly Review.

Why it persists

Structural, not behavioral. Dealer balance-sheet capacity, regulatory capital charges on short-vol books (post-Volmageddon), and steady end-investor protection demand all keep VRP wide. It compresses, never disappears, and re-widens after every shock — including 2018-Q1 and 2020-Q1.

Honest disclosure: the 12% DD claim CAN be breached

From EXP-3200's verdict object: claim_holds_all_scenarios: false. Specifically:

  • Baseline: 2.83% of paths breach 12% (i.e., 1-in-35).
  • Credit freeze (2008-style correlation collapse): 7.63% of paths breach.
  • Grinding drawdown (2022-style μ-shift −20%/yr): 97.69% of paths breach — i.e., the 12% claim does not hold in this regime.
  • Vol explosion (VIX 20 → 80, 2020-style): 100% of paths breach; mean DD 47%, worst 94%. This is the survival-of-the-firm scenario.
  • Worst p99 DD across all scenarios: 78.14% (vol_explosion, no circuit). The CB pulls this down but cannot eliminate it.

Our 12% target is a baseline-regime sizing target, not a guarantee. We size to it, hedge with the v5 sleeve, and circuit-break to it; we accept <18% as "tolerable but investigate", and treat anything >25% as a strategy-validity event triggering full external review and a Tranche step-down. These thresholds are written into our paper-to-live gates.

Non-exhaustive risk factors

  1. VRP compression risk. Structural conditions could change (regulatory rollback of capital charges on dealer short vol). Mitigant: 81% of edge is in non-SPX streams.
  2. Vol explosion / correlation collapse. EXP-3200 vol_explosion scenario breaches 12% DD on 100% of paths. The strategy survives but with severe drawdown. Mitigant: v5_hedge + 3% circuit breaker + pause-and-review at 25% DD.
  3. Grinding drawdown regime. EXP-3200 grinding_dd (2022-style negative drift) breaches 12% on 97.7% of paths. Mitigant: 6-month Sharpe regression triggers tranche step-down.
  4. Backtest decay risk. The 3.0–4.2 expected-live Sharpe band assumes k = 0.5–0.7. If realized k is 0.3, live Sharpe falls to ~1.5 and the program is paused.
  5. Rule Zero data risk. 7 of 8 capacity candidates lack option chains in IronVault. Phase 8 capacity assumes successful data unlock (Polygon / CBOE).
  6. Operational risk. Single-PM-key risk until Tranche 6; venue concentration risk until multi-venue routing is fully production.
  7. Distributional caveat in EXP-3200. MVN assumption — no Student-t, no jump diffusion. True tails may be thicker than reported.
