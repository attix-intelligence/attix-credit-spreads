Methodology & honest caveats

  • SPY weekly delta-hedged short straddle: every ISO week (first IronVault snapshot), find the put expiration closest to 7 DTE. Sell the ATM put + call for their real IronVault closes. Daily delta hedge: at each business-day close between entry and expiration, invert σ from real call + put closes, compute net Δ, and mark Δ_prev × (spot_t − spot_{t-1}) as the hedge leg. Exit = real option closes on the expiration date (or intrinsic if no late close is recorded).
  • Put credit spreads (XLF / XLI): weekly entry on first IronVault snapshot of each week, ~30 DTE, short strike at Δ ≈ -0.3, long strike at Δ ≈ -0.15. Position size = 2% of capital against max loss. Exit at expiration (or the last recorded close before).
  • QQQ BLOCKED: IronVault carries only 99 sparse QQQ snapshots ending 2023. Weekly strategies need ≥5× more snapshot density. The same OCC-construction path used for the TLT Dec 2025 backfill would restore QQQ.
  • IWM BLOCKED: zero IronVault contracts. Needs a Polygon Starter backfill from scratch. Not fudged with a synthetic substitute — Rule Zero.
  • Delta hedge approximation: daily discrete rehedge, not continuous. Realised gamma P&L is partially captured. A minute-bar delta hedge would reduce slippage further but needs intraday data IronVault does not store.
  • Correlation to EXP-1220 is yearly (n≤6). Directional only, not statistically significant.
  • Capacity: SPY/XLF/XLI ATM options routinely trade $1 B+/day in notional; these sleeves would not hit participation caps at production book sizes that break the GLD/SLV calendar at $50-80M. Capacity is the WHOLE POINT of this experiment — even a lower-Sharpe stream on liquid names can beat a higher-Sharpe stream that caps out at $80M.
