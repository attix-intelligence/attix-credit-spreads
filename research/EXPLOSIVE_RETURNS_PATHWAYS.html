Pathway 1 — Solana On-Chain Arbitrage / MEV

Theoretical edge

  • Triangular DEX arbitrage across Raydium / Orca / Meteora / Lifinity. Edge source: order-flow imbalance + AMM curve fragmentation.
  • Sandwich / JIT liquidity on Jupiter aggregator routes. Edge: latency advantage and mempool visibility.
  • CEX – DEX latency arbitrage: Binance/Coinbase spot vs Solana DEX pools. Edge: market segmentation.

Underlying microstructure: bid-ask spread capture, not statistical edge. Edge per opportunity is small (1 – 30 bps), high-frequency.

Realistic annual return

CapitalRealistic net CAGRNotes
$10K50 – 200%Below institutional searcher minimum; priority fees consume 30 – 60% of gross
$100K30 – 100%Mid-tier searchers; Jito tippers compress edge
$1M10 – 40%Top-searcher tier; Jito MEV revenue across all searchers ~$300M/yr — capturing >0.5% solo is rare

Sources: Eigenphi MEV dashboards, Jito searcher leaderboards, Flashbots research (Ethereum analogue), Frontier Research 2024 Solana MEV report.

Max drawdown risk

  • Smart contract exploit on a pool used in route: 100% loss of capital parked in that pool (Cypher, Mango Markets 2022 – 2023).
  • Rug pulls on niche pools used for multi-hop routes.
  • Toxic flow: a faster bot can flip gross negative within hours.
  • Bridge exploits if cross-chain (Wormhole, Nomad, Multichain — ~$1B combined losses).
  • Realistic max DD: 30 – 80% over any 12 months for a small operator. Public reporting hides this via survivor bias.

AUM capacity

  • Hard ceiling ~$5M working capital for a single operator on Solana DEX arb. Above this, available arb sizes (pool depth $50K – $500K) become a small share of capital.
  • $5M – $50M possible only by building a competing searcher operation against incumbents (Jito-Solana validators, BloXroute, Manifold) with multi-year R&D leads.

Infrastructure

  • Dedicated Solana RPC: Helius / QuickNode / Triton ($1K – $5K/mo dedicated, $20K+/mo co-located).
  • Jito-Solana validator or Block Engine bundle relay access (revenue-share or ~$1M+ SOL stake to run validator).
  • Rust searcher bot: 6 – 12 months engineering for a competitive bot.
  • Capital float ~$50K – $200K across pools and CEX hot wallets.
  • Redundant geographic deployment + 24/7 on-call.

Regulatory and operational risks

  • OFAC/SDN counterparty screening required; failure = sanctions exposure.
  • MEV ethics / PR risk; growing protocol-level mitigations (e.g., Jupiter shielded mode).
  • IRS: every arb cycle is a taxable event in the US; thousands per day = bookkeeping problem.
  • Bank de-risking of crypto-active accounts.
  • No customer recourse if exchange or bridge fails (FTX precedent).

Rule Zero data sourcing checklist

  1. Eigenphi / Flashbots historical MEV dataset (Solana): priority fee, gross, net per opportunity.
  2. Jito bundle submission history (public via Jito API).
  3. Raydium / Orca / Meteora pool history (indexable via Helius archive RPC).
  4. Reconstruct realistic competitive fill rates (not theoretical opportunity counts) — this is the killer assumption most retail MEV backtests get wrong.
Verdict. Higher gross plausible at $10K. Risk-adjusted return is far worse than v8a (Sharpe 0.5 – 2.5 vs 6.39). Drawdown includes a total-loss tail (smart contract exploit). Recommend: do not pursue.

Pathway 2 — Crypto Volatility / Basis Arbitrage

Sub-strategies: perp-spot funding arb · CEX – DEX basis · term-structure basis (Deribit dated vs perp) · realized-vs-implied vol on Deribit DVOL.

Theoretical edge

Structural, not speculative. Funding rate is the clearing mechanism for derivatives demand. When perps trade premium (most bull regimes), funding is positive → cash-and-carry short perp + long spot. Historical BTC/ETH perp funding has averaged 10 – 30% APR in 2021 – 2024 (Glassnode, Binance Futures, Coinglass).

Edge is persistent because it reflects retail leverage demand, which is unlikely to vanish.

Realistic annual return

CapitalRealistic net CAGRNotes
$10K15 – 40%Constrained by minimum trade sizes and fee drag
$100K15 – 35%Institutional tiers unlock at $100K+ (50 – 80% fee reduction)
$1M10 – 25%Meaningful share of OI on tier-2 venues; needs venue diversification

Sharpe historical (Galaxy Digital, 21Shares, Amberdata): 1.5 – 3.5 for delta-neutral basis books 2021 – 2024 — below v8a's 6.39.

Max drawdown risk

  • Exchange counterparty failure: FTX (Nov 2022), Mt. Gox, BitMEX OFAC settlement (2020). Unhedgeable.
  • Liquidation cascades on perp side during fast moves.
  • Stablecoin depeg: USDC to $0.87 in March 2023 (SVB exposure); USDT multiple <$0.95 events.
  • Regulatory shutdown: Binance US, Kraken (SEC, Feb 2023), Coinbase (SEC suit, Jun 2023).
  • Realistic max DD: 15 – 30% normal; 50 – 100% tail on venue failure.

AUM capacity

  • Per-venue: $50M – $500M depending on OI / ADV. Top-3 venues each absorb $200M+.
  • Aggregate $1B+ plausible across 5+ venues with redundancy.
  • Edge has compressed from 25%+ APR in 2021 bull to single digits in chop — regime-dependent.

Infrastructure

  • API access to 3 – 5 venues (Binance, Bybit, OKX, Deribit, Hyperliquid).
  • CCXT or direct WebSocket (~2 – 4 weeks engineering).
  • Cross-margin collateral manager (critical, non-trivial).
  • KYC / institutional onboarding 2 – 8 weeks per venue; $100K – $1M for institutional tiers.
  • No co-location required; latency matters only for liquidation defense.

Regulatory and operational risks

  • US persons restricted from offshore perp venues (CFTC enforcement on BitMEX, Binance). US fund must use Coinbase Derivatives, CME, or Kraken Futures — significantly worse funding terms.
  • CFTC vs SEC perp classification unsettled.
  • Tax: funding receipts = ordinary income (not 1256 60/40 like v8a).
  • Stablecoin issuer regulatory exposure (Circle, Tether).
  • SEC custody rule applies if any portion represents customer assets.

Rule Zero data sourcing checklist

  1. Binance Futures funding rate history (free API, 8-hourly back to 2019).
  2. Coinglass aggregator for cross-venue funding (free tier sufficient).
  3. Deribit DVOL and option chain (free CSV back to 2020).
  4. Glassnode on-chain stablecoin metrics for liquidity context.
  5. Realistic execution model: taker fees (4 – 6 bps), maker rebates (-1 to -2 bps), 8h funding ticks, stablecoin transfer latency.
Verdict. The only pathway with a structural edge, clean data, and tractable infra cost. Realistic 15 – 25% net CAGR at $100K – $1M with Sharpe 1.5 – 3.0 — inferior to v8a on risk-adjusted basis but potentially uncorrelated. Counterparty tail is the dominant risk and is not insurable. Recommend: small Rule-Zero feasibility experiment using free Binance funding history. Cap research budget at one cycle.

Pathway 3 — 0DTE SPX Options (Short Gamma)

Theoretical edge

Variance risk premium concentrated in last 0 – 1 day to expiry. Vasquez et al. (2025, SSRN 5113405): 0DTE SPX volume ~59% of total SPX. Dim, Eraker, Vilkov (2023, SSRN 4692190): market-maker net gamma on 0DTE is positive and dampens realized vol. Terstegge (2025): residual VRP after the 2010s decline materializes overnight on SPX puts; intraday 0DTE has a smaller but high-frequency premium.

Edge type: volatility premium harvesting, not bid-ask spread capture.

Realistic annual return

CapitalRealistic net CAGRNotes
$10K10 – 30%PDT rule; assignment risk; small wing structures
$100K20 – 50%Iron condors / broken-wing flies; lit Sharpe 0.8 – 1.5
$1M15 – 35%Tail-dominated; portfolio margin needed; one bad day can erase a quarter

Sources: TastyTrade research (2023 – 2024), CBOE 0DTE papers, Bondarenko-Bernardi (2024) intraday VRP, ORATS practitioner data.

Max drawdown risk

  • Aug 5, 2024 SPX -3% intraday: 0DTE short-vol books lost 15 – 40% in a single session (ORATS / practitioner postmortems).
  • Feb 5, 2018 (VIXmageddon): XIV terminated, LJM Preservation -80%+ in one day. Not 0DTE but the closest short-vol analog.
  • OptionSellers.com (Nov 2018): short naked strangles wiped accounts to negative balances. Recurring failure mode for unhedged short-gamma.
  • Realistic max DD: 20 – 40% in defined-risk wing structures; 50 – 80%+ tail without proper wings.

AUM capacity

  • Very large: 0DTE SPX notional >$500B daily (CBOE 2024). Capacity $100M – $1B at retail-style sizing.
  • Edge has not visibly decayed through 2025 — Vasquez et al. show customer flow is two-sided, preserving VRP residual.

Infrastructure

  • Lowest of the three. IBKR Pro / TastyTrade / Schwab with portfolio margin ($200K minimum at most PB).
  • OPRA top-of-book ($200 – $1500/mo); CBOE LiveVol if backtest stack required (~$3K/mo).
  • No co-location, no private RPC, no custody concerns.
  • Position sizing engine with hard wing-width caps as % of NAV is critical.

Regulatory and operational risks

  • CBOE-listed, SEC + FINRA oversight, 1256 60/40 tax treatment (favorable).
  • FINRA 2024 – 2025 reports flagged retail 0DTE losses; future rule changes possible (margin, PDT, suitability).
  • No novel regulatory risk beyond what we already manage.
  • Operational risk is execution-quality driven, not custody/counterparty.

Rule Zero data sourcing checklist

  1. Verify IronVault options_cache.db SPX coverage at 0DTE; if absent, source from CBOE LiveVol or ORATS.
  2. Realistic fill model: 0DTE bid-ask wider in % than 28-DTE; measure effective vs quoted (Muravyev-Pearson 2020 methodology).
  3. Backtest must include Feb 2018, March 2020, Aug 2024 for tail calibration.
Verdict. Lowest infra barrier, well-regulated, tax-advantaged. But Sharpe is structurally lower than v8a (0.8 – 1.5 vs 6.39) and tail is worse (Aug 2024 precedent). Recommend: medium-priority feasibility candidate as a complementary stream — never replacement. Note: v8a already harvests VRP via 28 – 7 DTE SPY; the diversification benefit of adding 0 – 1 DTE SPX must be measured, not assumed.

Recommendation

Pathway 1

Do not pursue

Risk-adjusted return materially worse than v8a; infrastructure cost prohibitive at our scale; tail risk operationally toxic. Public success stories heavily survivor-biased.

Pathway 2

Single feasibility experiment

Use free Binance funding history. Validate ≥10% net CAGR with Sharpe ≥1.5 after realistic fees. If yes, consider $50K – $100K satellite later — never replace v8a.

Pathway 3

Cheap to test if data exists

Most natural extension of our competence. Empirical question: does it add independent return on top of v8a's 28 – 7 DTE SPY? Cheap if IronVault has SPX 0DTE; else ~$3K data spend.

Headline finding. v8a's 6.39 Sharpe / 118% CAGR / 5.1% DD is not casually beaten by any of the three pathways. The "explosive returns in days/weeks" framing is largely survivorship bias of crypto cycles and viral options trades; the durable, replicable strategies in each space have Sharpe well below v8a.

The most reliable way to add explosive expected return is not to swap to a worse-Sharpe strategy — it is to lever v8a within its drawdown budget. At 5.1% max DD, there is room for additional vol-targeting beyond current overlays. That belongs in a separate experiment (EXP-3320 candidate), not in chasing crypto.
