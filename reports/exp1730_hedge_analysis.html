EXP-1730 as unpurchased hedge

EXP-1730 is structurally different from purchased insurance: it is a return-generating strategy (not a drag). The question isn't "how much does it cost?" but "does it contribute positive returns when equity strategies suffer?"

  • Standalone CAGR: essentially 0% (14-year break-even)
  • Stress-month contribution: $-243 across 8 stress months
  • Normal-month contribution: $-192 across 30 normal months
  • Yearly correlation to equity strategy: -0.656 (moderate)

Verdict: EXP-1730 does NOT deliver positive returns during stress months. It is not a hedge in the practical sense — it's just an uncorrelated break-even strategy.
