Capacity Bottleneck

The binding constraint is GLD/TLT options liquidity — ADV of 15K-25K contracts per strike vs SPY's 200K+. At $1B AUM, the cross-asset pairs strategy would consume 5%+ of daily volume, causing 50+ bps of market impact per trade. The tail risk overlay (95% of capital) runs through SPY/VIX options which can absorb billions with minimal impact.

6. Cost Model Specification

6a. Bid-Ask Spread

Half-spread crossed on each leg entry and exit: cost = legs × contracts × half_spread × $100 × 2

6b. Market Impact (Almgren-Chriss)

impact = η × √(participation) × 10,000 + γ × participation × 10,000

Where: η = 0.10 (temporary), γ = 0.05 (permanent), participation = order_contracts / ADV

6c. Slippage Model

slippage = half_spread × (1 + 5 × √participation) × urgency_mult

Urgency multipliers: low=0.5x, medium=1.0x, high=1.5x, critical=3.0x

6d. Margin Cost

annual_cost = (leverage - 1.0) × AUM × 5.5%

At 1.6x leverage: borrow 60% of equity at 5.5% = 3.3% annual drag on total AUM

6e. Rebalancing Cost

annual = 52 × (AUM × 2% drift × N_strategies × 0.5) × 12 bps

Weekly rebalance assuming 2% average weight drift per cycle across 5 strategies

7. Scaling Recommendations

$1M–$10M: Full Alpha Capture

Negligible market impact (<1 bps). All costs from margin borrowing (3.3%) and spreads. Net CAGR retains >90% of gross. Optimal operating range for the current strategy mix.

$10M–$100M: Moderate Impact

GLD/TLT options start showing impact (5-15 bps per trade). Consider splitting orders across multiple expirations and using TWAP execution. Net CAGR still comfortably above 40%.

$100M–$500M: Requires Structural Changes

Must diversify execution across more instruments. Consider: (1) adding more pairs beyond GLD-TLT to distribute flow, (2) trading weekly options to access more expirations, (3) using dark pools for large orders. The SPY/VIX leg handles this scale easily.

$500M–$1B: Alpha Erosion Zone

Market impact on the less-liquid legs (GLD, TLT, XLF) erodes 5-15% of gross CAGR. The strategy remains profitable but Sharpe drops significantly. To scale further: restructure away from illiquid options toward futures or swap-based execution.
